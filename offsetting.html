CARBON OFFSETTING


Carbon offset schemes were set up to allow the largest polluters who exceed permitted emissions’ levels to fund projects, such as reforestation, that reduce carbon dioxide in the air, essentially balancing out their emissions equation. The types of carbon offset projects that are implemented are diverse. They range from forestry sequestration projects (which remove CO2 from the atmosphere when trees grow) to energy efficiency and renewable energy projects (which reduce future CO2 emissions in the atmosphere).
Businesses can achieve carbon neutral or even carbon net negative as long as they are willing to pay the price. A relatively small offsetting market has recently seen a fresh boom, that since autumn in 2019 a lot of new demand has occurred, and the current remaining credits will not be able to meet that demand very soon. Hundreds of companies are offering such schemes around the world.
In Scotland, there are 20000 hectares of peatland restoration and 12000 hectares of woodland created every year (The Scottish Government, 2018). This shows massive potential of carbon offsetting, yet only a fraction developed as carbon offsetting programmes. According to our calculation, the total carbon offsetting capacity in Scotland should be more than 7 million tonnes of CO2e per year (Forestry and Land Scotland, 2020b).
In terms of accreditation, verification, locality and lagged effects due to time consumed for trees to grow or peatland to be restored, we managed to identify 3 programmes for businesses to participate.
(a) Plan Vivo
(b) Trees 4 Life
(c) Forestry and Land Scotland
