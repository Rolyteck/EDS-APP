Pledge

"As Small and/or Medium Enterprises in Scotland, We declare our commitment to support the scottish government to transition to a Net zero economy by 2030 and sustainably mitigate our emissions by calculating our carbon footprint and signing up to at least 5 low carbon activity annually."

Carbon Footprinting

A Carbon Footprint can be defined as a measure of:
"The total greenhouse gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by a person, organisation, event or product" (Carbon Trust, 2018).
The GHG emissions required to be measured under a carbon footprint were also set out under the Kyoto Protocol:
Carbon Dioxide (?C0?_2)
Methane (CH_4)
Nitrous Oxide (N_2 O)
Sulphur Hexafluoride (?SF?_6)
Hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs)
Perfluorocarbons (PFCs)

Reasons/Benefits for Carbon Measurement
a. Cost Reduction.
b. Consistency with Trending Expectation.
c. Helps with Disclosure to Investors.
d. Regulatory Compliance.
d. Risk Management Strategies are built.
Steps to Measure Carbon Footprint:
i. Set Organizational Boundary.
ii. Set Operational Boundary (scope 1,2&3).
iii. Define Time-period which is supposed to correspond to the financial year.
iv. Collect Activity Data, apply emissions Factor and convert.

Monitoring & Evaluation.

Edinburgh Science is Partnering with Environmental Management System (EMS), such as Green Ticks, would be most suitable for the second mandatory commitment. Green Ticks, a local, Edinburgh based service established by Bright Green Business, works with SMEs to establish EMS in accordance with British Standards, making an otherwise difficult process easy and efficient. This will be checked on an annual basis by the Bright Green Business company and adds another dimension to the scheme that will continue to engage business in different aspects of climate change. EMS include core elements of typical management structures, covering organisation structure, planning activities, responsibilities and procedures.


Reporting & Certification

Once these commitments signed up to have been verified and reported, the signatory should receive an email with a pledge certificate that will be recognised in Scotland as a Green business that is transitioning to a low-carbon, and this would not only make Scotland more sustainable, but also attract customers and investors.
Following this, to ensure engagement continues throughout the project we advise Edinburgh Science host various annual workshops/forums, which also will be expanded upon later in greater detail.
